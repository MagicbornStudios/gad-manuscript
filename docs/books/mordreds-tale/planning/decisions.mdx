# Mordred's Tale — Decisions

| Id | Status | Summary | Consequence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `MT-STYLE-DIALOGUE-FIRST` | `accepted` | Newer pages favor dialogue and interiority over long scenic description; enough setting to orient | Each page should have at least one exchange or reaction and one moment of interiority |
| `MT-SANDERSON-ADJACENT` | `accepted` | Tone and structure lean Sanderson-like: clear cause-effect, character-driven choices, propulsive plot | Magic and power feel rule-bound; character goals and costs stay visible |
| `MT-TIMELINE-JACK-IS-PAST` | `accepted` | Jack's chapter is the past (Ch2); it leads up to Morgana's current predicament. The woman sleeping in the lair during Jack's time is Morgana. | Ch2 = past; Ch1/Ch3 = present. **Tarro** rises on the secret of the sleeping Morgana and the wells that follow. |
| `MT-CH3-INTERCUT-REALIGN` | `accepted` | Ch3 intercuts Morgana’s present (she’s taken) with Jack’s “not the same time” timeline; by end of **Act I** (last beat of Ch3), Jack re-aligns into the present and meets again. | Act I ends with convergence + “Morgana taken” + set-up for Act II orphanage route. |
| `MT-CH3-JACK-DATELINE` | `accepted` | Jack’s lair scenes in Ch3 are **flashbacks**: he leaves the lair and surfaces **before** Morgana’s eastern-quarters / imprisonment present thread. Datelines must say **earlier**, not **later**, so readers do not read Jack as still underground during Morgana’s present. | Intercut labels and section kicker copy stay chronologically honest; see Ch3 page-74 bridge. |
| `MT-CH3-MORGANA-TAKEN-MORDRED-ORPHANAGE` | `accepted` | Morgana is taken during Ch3; Mordred is separated to the **House of Small Mercies** as an **infant**; Jack’s **network** closes the hunt by Act I’s last beat (`MT-JACK-PRE-CH3-LAIR-RHYTHM`). | Act II opens orphanage + bark / Midas beats. |
| `MT-FINISH-DRAFT-FIRST` | `accepted` | Get the full manuscript drafted; polish later. Content over polish. | Draft phase first; mt-polish deferred. |
| `MT-CHAPTER-AT-A-TIME` | `accepted` | Execution mode is chapter-at-a-time: draft full chapter, then one chapter-level rework pass before moving on. | Prevents page-level perfectionism from stalling completion. |
| `MT-CHAPTER-DONE-CHECKLIST` | `accepted` | A chapter is only marked done when the Chapter Done Checklist passes (beats landed, causality, motives, canon continuity, handoff, read-through, build check). | Gives objective completion gate for progress tracking. |
| `MT-MAGIC-NOT-FREEFLOWING-CH3` | `accepted` | In Ch3 / Act I, magic has not kicked back to free-flowing. People have magical gear (relics) but not the old free flow. | **Tarro’s** edge is powered by hidden wells — sleeping Morgana, then Morgana+Mordred, then **Enid** (see `MT-MORDRED-MAGIC-WELL`). Reason for Jack to go deeper (Merlin) when needed. |
| `MT-MORGANA-POWERLESS-WITHOUT-CHILD` | `accepted` | Morgana is **powerless without** Mordred in reach (or when separated); Mordred is the **well** she conducts (`MT-MORDRED-MAGIC-WELL`). | Ch3 vulnerability + separation stakes. |
| `MT-MERLIN-AT-CORE` | `accepted` | Deeper in the lair: core of the tree. Merlin there, stopped the tree's growth from within; doesn't know time passed. Opening that area kick-starts free-flowing magic in the world. Merlin thought it was "just an experiment." | Big story beat: when opened, magic returns globally; Jack (or others) have reason to go deeper. |
| `MT-HALF-SISTER` | `accepted` | Mordred’s sibling is his **half-sister** — created by the empire using Morgana + a subordinate (same method as Jack in Ch2). Raised by Subordinate’s family; used as a tool to power relics until Merlin (~14). | Act II–III; she hates Jack and the empire after. |
| `MT-SUBORDINATE-ACT-I` | `accepted` | We **meet the subordinate** in Act I — Jack’s **right-hand man**; he will father the half-sister with Morgana. **Morally grey** compared to Jack; doesn’t always believe in Jack’s methods but does as he is told. | Empire and half-sister set-up; contrast with Jack. |
| `MT-DEFAULT-CHARACTER-NAMES` | `accepted` | **Working names** (Arthurian/medieval flavor): Subordinate = **Owain**; half-sister = **Enid**; her daughter = **Gwyneth**. Docs may still say “Subordinate / half-sister / daughter” for role. | Prose and dialogue use Owain, Enid, Gwyneth; cast table on [state](/docs/books/mordreds-tale/planning/state). |
| `MT-SIBLING-NOT-BORN-YET` | `accepted` | At Mordred’s orphanage placement (Act I end), the half-sister has not been born yet. | Act II opens Mordred-alone; half-sister thread begins after her birth. |
| `MT-HALF-SISTER-RAISED-BY-SUBORDINATE` | `accepted` | The half-sister is **raised by the Subordinate’s family** (not directly by Jack). | She has a “house” and status; arranged marriage follows. |
| `MT-ARRANGED-MARRIAGE-GENERAL` | `accepted` | By the time of the castle meeting (Act II), the half-sister is **arranged to be married to a general** in Jack’s army. | Stakes and position in empire; can inform the hallway scene. |
| `MT-CASTLE-MEETING-AGES` | `accepted` | Their **initial meeting** (castle hallway) is when Mordred is **15** and the half-sister is **14**. | Lock ages for that scene. |
| `MT-MERLIN-WHEN-HALF-SISTER-14` | `accepted` | **Merlin is found at the end of Act II** when the **half-sister is still 14** — roughly around when Mordred and she meet the first time. Jack discovers him at the core; opening it kick-starts magic, tree grows, magic fills the world; empire threatened. That discovery is the **act break** closing Act II. | Not 30 years after Act I; Act II spans to ~age 15/14. |
| `MT-ACT-III-FAST-FORWARD` | `accepted` | **Act III fast-forwards** from the end of Act II to the conflict. We jump to when the southern war is underway; Mordred and his half-sister **remeet** there (with her daughter). | Act III does not span the full gap; it opens in the conflict era. |
| `MT-HALF-SISTER-POWER-SOURCE` | `accepted` | After Morgana is taken, Jack maintains power by using the **half-sister** (via touch) to charge relics — same as sleeping Morgana — until Merlin is found (when she is ~14). | She is both character and resource until the act break. |
| `MT-TONE-MORALLY-GREY` | `accepted` | Audience should feel **morally implicated**; tone favours **morally grey decisions**, **abrupt rash decisions**, **betrayal**, **submissiveness**, **resistance**, **greed**, **karma**, **scheming**. No clean heroes; consequences land. | Unsettled, hope and dread mixed. |
| `MT-MAGICBORN-SERIES-FEEL` | `accepted` | **Magicborn-line** target feel: **darker**, more **shocking**, high **emotion**, **tragic** stakes, with characters still **powerful** and **surviving** — not nihilism for its own sake. **Moral dilemmas**, **ugly choices**, and **controversial dialogue** are welcome when they serve **cost**, **survivorship**, and **cause–effect** (see `MT-SANDERSON-ADJACENT`). | Beat workshops and draft passes explicitly ask: what is the **jeopardy**, the **taboo**, the **price**, and who **walks out scarred but alive**? |
| `MT-BOOK1-BEATS-PASS-BEFORE-CH3` | `accepted` | Book 1 **execution order**: run **`mt-beats-pass-01`** (beat-lock **Ch6–14** + closeout + FQ sweep) **before** `mt-act1-02` / `mt-act1-03`. **Ch4–5** chapter rework runs **after** beat closeout so the Act II–III spine matches locked choices; **Ch3** returns **last** among these gates so clay-cord / separation can align with the full forward map. | Ch4 rework may note **Ch3 bridges TBD** until Ch3 pass; registry dependencies follow [task registry](/docs/books/mordreds-tale/planning/task-registry). |
| `MT-CH6-POV-OWAIN` | `accepted` | **Chapter 6 — *The Second Well*:** tight third on **Owain** (guilt, “hero” rot, institutional self-justification). Morgana’s interior may surface only as **Owain reads her** (face, breath, refusal) — not her POV. | Ch6 draft and rework stay in Owain’s lens unless a later pass explicitly revisits. |
| `MT-MORGANA-SILENCE-OWAIN-COERCION` | `accepted` | In the **Owain–Morgana / second-well** thread, Morgana does **not** speak to Owain — **no** curse, **no** bargain line, **no** scripted plea that gives him a “dialogue” to hide behind. Silence is **resistance**; any speech to **others** later is unchanged by this lock. | Ch6 avoids the “one killer line from Morgana” beat; cost lives in **what Owain does** and **what he cannot un-know**. |
| `MT-TANYA-SUSPECTS-NEVER-FULL-KNOWLEDGE` | `accepted` | **Tanya** never gets **full** confirmation of Owain’s Jack/Morgana complicity (second well, keys, what happens on those nights). Suspicion **sharpens** after a **pattern**: Owain **gone most nights** for a **while** — timing, body, lies, warmth gone — and that **suspected-not-known** gap stays a **domestic horror** engine. | Ch6+ home beats: **subtext**, not confession; no full reveal to Tanya. |
| `MT-ENID-COVER-STORY-JACK-TO-TANYA` | `accepted` | When **Enid** enters Owain’s household, the **story to Tanya** (and the plausible public face) is that Enid is **Jack’s bastard**; **Owain** raises her because **Jack will not bother with fathering** / doesn’t want the bother. **Biological truth** remains **Owain + Morgana** per `MT-MORGANA-COUNCIL-COLLUSION` / `MT-HALF-SISTER`. | Aligns with `MT-TANYA-OWAIN-HOUSEHOLD` for Enid’s childhood fear of Tanya; Tanya’s **resentment/suspicion** can target the **Jack** story as well as Owain’s absences. |
| `MT-CH6-COERCION-PROCEDURE-EXPLICIT` | `accepted` | **Ch6 — second well:** combine **procedure/consequence** framing (keys, orders, Jack as **auditor**, Owain’s interior split, marriage cost) with **explicit** on-page **weight** where it **lands cost** (GoT-adjacent: reader cannot pretend harm is abstract). **Not** a mandate for **gore** or **splatter** — see `MT-BOOK1-VIOLENCE-CONSEQUENCE-OVER-GORE`. | Draft Ch6 with **institutional + visceral clarity**; avoid **titillation** and **torture rhythm**. |
| `MT-BOOK1-VIOLENCE-CONSEQUENCE-OVER-GORE` | `accepted` | Book 1 **preference:** **consequence**, **hard decisions**, **moral dilemmas**, and **irrational in-the-moment** choices that **spark conflict** over **extended gore** or **viscera spectacle**. Shock comes from **price**, **choice**, and **aftermath**, not from **lingering** wound porn. | Battle and horror beats stay **cost-forward**; explicit sex/violence when present serves **character and politics**, not **gratuity**. |
| `MT-CH7-POV-MORDRED` | `accepted` | **Chapter 7 — *Devil in the Register*:** tight third on **Mordred** (toddler → young child cognition). Use **Merris**-adjacent focal only for short **institutional** glimpses if the child cannot parse a beat; default stays **Mordred**. | Matches Ch4 child POV pattern; keeps church/registry language as **heard** and **felt**, not explained. |
| `MT-CH7-JACK-GRILLE-NEAR-MISS` | `accepted` | **Jack** does **not** fully reunite with Mordred in Ch7. He **sees** the boy **through a grille** (or equivalent barrier) — **near miss**, **blocked**, **no** warm reconciliation; silence or near-silence on both sides is allowed. **Not** Alt A (letters only). | Preserves Jack as **threat** and **almost**-father; fuels later beats without burning the Act II arc early. |
| `MT-CH7-NO-ESCAPE-ATTEMPT` | `accepted` | Ch7 does **not** use Alternative C (**failed escape**). Stakes rise through **registry**, **language**, **bark**, **punishment**, and **whispers** — not a **prison-break** beat here. | Escape attempts stay available for a **later** chapter if ever; avoids **burning** tension too early (`MT-CH7` beat sheet warning). |
| `MT-FQ-01-REGISTRY-CHURCH-WARD-JACK-PATRONAGE` | `accepted` | On **House / church paperwork**, Mordred is a **church–institution ward** (House of Small Mercies / pantheon-channel registry). **Jack’s** control shows as **patronage**, **favors**, **funding**, or **shell arrangements** — **not** a clean “father” line on the form that fixes everything. Crown may appear on **seals** or **tax** edges without making the child “royal property” in daily life. | Resolves `FQ-01` for drafting; keeps **legal murk** for conflict and **Merris** leverage. |
| `MT-CH7-MERRIS-PROTECTOR-AND-DISCIPLINARIAN` | `accepted` | **Sister Merris** is **not** purely savior or purely villain: she **shields** Mordred from **worse** outside predators when it serves the House, and **disciplines** / **extracts** him institutionally (food, isolation, bark, registry). **Dilemma** beats over cartoon evil. | Ch7: punishment and “care” can **contradict** without resolving — [`MT-BOOK1-VIOLENCE-CONSEQUENCE-OVER-GORE`](/docs/books/mordreds-tale/planning/decisions). |
| `MT-CH8-POV-OWAIN` | `accepted` | **Chapter 8 — *Geraint’s Sister*:** tight third on **Owain** for the birth-in-household, **Jack** visits, **Tanya** poison, **Geraint** jealousy seeds, **bark** on **Enid**, and guided **metal** touch; **Tanya** horror and **Enid** interiority surface only as Owain reads them unless a later pass adds a deliberate split thread. | Aligns with Ch6 home arc and Owain’s split-love fracture; reserves **Enid**-limited child POV for later beats per roadmap. |
| `MT-OWAIN-RELIC-COVER-VOCAB` | `accepted` | Owain’s **lie to Tanya** (and self-justification) uses **real** Tarro gate jargon: **delver** roster, **registry** stamps, **charge-sensitive** convoy, **sealed-tongue** / chapel witness, **patron priority** night slots — same vocabulary as legitimate **relic-hunter** work, wrong referent for the cell nights. | Ch6+ domestic scenes; keeps the mask credible and morally worse. |
| `MT-LENGTH` | `accepted` | ~250–300 words per page; pages per chapter flexible (scene-driven); novel target ~80k–100k words. | Fix in draft or polish. |
| `MT-ACT-III-MORDRED-KING` | `accepted` | Act III: **Mordred overtakes Jack and Jack’s empire easily** and becomes the **new king**. Origin of Magicborn. | How he does it is a focus of Act III. |
| `MT-ACT-III-BATTLE` | `accepted` | **Fierce battle** between Mordred and half-sister and her daughter. She fights with spells first (assumes he has better relics — correct — but he also has **overwhelming magical ability**); he drops armor and casts; onlookers see similar spells, different speech, spells vary. She **disrobes top half** to remove **bark** suppressing her magic; **whirlwind scars** on back and front torso; uses rings, then **weird speech** — **fireballs and wind sickles**; he deflects with rings, maneuvers past wind sickle. **Mordred’s torso**: **more whirlwind scars** than his sister’s. Then he shows **true power**, **turns the tide easily**; challengers hit him and his caravan **here and there**; **majority of Jack’s army destroyed** by his **final spells**; he **lets the neighboring kingdom continue its invasion**. | Act III summary on [state](/docs/books/mordreds-tale/planning/state). |
| `MT-ACT-III-CARRIAGE` | `accepted` | Neighboring kingdom’s army **escorts Mordred to Jack’s castle** with half-sister. **Carriage scene:** Mordred, half-sister, daughter **talk on the way**. She **hates Jack**, **Subordinate** (her father per Act I), and **the empire** — she was a **tool to power relics**. She reveals she was a **military tool** for **stronger relic-wielding** than average; more about **herself and her daughter** to Mordred. **Act III reveal: Jack is the father of the daughter** (not half-sister’s father). | After she witnesses his battlefield power. |
| `MT-JACK-FATHERS-DAUGHTER` | `accepted` | **Jack** is the **biological father** of the **half-sister’s 15-year-old daughter**. The **half-sister** is still fathered by the **Subordinate** (Act I). Paternity of the daughter is **revealed in Act III** (carriage, approach to castle, or other beat). | Explains empire politics, hatred, and stakes. |
| `MT-CASTLE-MEETING` | `accepted` | Act II key scene: Mordred wanders into a hallway in Jack’s castle; half-sister sees a boy that didn’t belong, with her guards. Either (a) conflict — she gets in trouble for Mordred’s doing, or (b) brief dialogue before Mordred escapes/leaves. | Pick or blend when drafting. |
| `MT-KINGDOM-OF-TARRO` | `accepted` | Jack’s order is not a separate “empire” in kind — it is the **Kingdom of Tarro**, one kingdom among many. Tarro and its church **regulate access** to the Pantheon lair (the tree) and thus to relics once magic returns; southern rivals fight a **resource war** against that gate. | Public language: Tarro; geopolitics for Act III southern conflict. |
| `MT-RELIC-MARKET-ACT-I` | `accepted` | **Anyone** may own relics in law; in practice **black markets** and Jack’s brokerage move charged pieces for **coin, favors, and position**. Relic charge is **time-based** (it runs down and must be renewed). By Ch3 there is **no ambient free-flowing magic** — only what relics still hold — though living memory expects the old world. | Aligns economy chapters with “borrowed” charge and scarcity. |
| `MT-JACK-PRE-CH3-LAIR-RHYTHM` | `accepted` | Jack **escaped the lair months before** Ch3’s present. He **returns often** to charge relics and scout for new ones. **One day Morgana is gone** when he checks; Ch3’s hunt is him tracing her through **connections** until, by Act I’s last beat, **his network delivers her** again. | Ch3 Jack thread: search + convergence; dateline rules still apply for flashbacks vs present. |
| `MT-OWAIN-WANTS-HEROISM` | `accepted` | Owain’s private hunger is to be seen (and to see himself) as a **hero**, not merely a competent enforcer. | Shades his compliance, envy, and the Enid household drama. |
| `MT-PANTHEON-POPULAR-DREAD` | `accepted` | Culturally, **almost everyone** is glad the old pantheon is gone: they remember (or were taught) **immortal cruelty**, favor-trading, and knees bent to power. Tarro’s newer generations did not live as slaves to gods; distance makes some **nostalgic for stability**, not the whip. | Church-of-pantheon in Tarro is **immigrant-founded** exception, not universal faith. |
| `MT-MORGANA-MERLIN-IMMORTAL-GIFT` | `accepted` | Morgana was born **mortal**, a **political gift** from another kingdom to **Merlin** — immortality crafted for **his pleasure** and for her kingdom’s favor. Her kingdom **did not survive the Fall** (chaos, revolt, poor rule); **neighboring realms took refugees**. She understands **why** she conceived Mordred despite her older sacrifice. | Locks backstory for interiority and political shame. |
| `MT-SURVIVING-POWERS-MERLIN-MORGANA` | `accepted` | After the Fall, **only Merlin and Morgana** remain of the old divine tier in practice — **Merlin in stasis** at the tree’s core until opened; **Morgana** awake in the world. The rest of the pantheon are **gone** for story purposes (dead, scattered, irrelevant). | Cosmology for Magicborn docs + manuscript. |
| `MT-MORDRED-MAGIC-WELL` | `accepted` | **Mordred is a living source (well)** of magic. While **Morgana holds him**, power **flows** in a way that can **recharge** relics “like before” for that window; mechanism stays **time-limited** on the objects. **Some** relics show charge state **visually**; not all. | Refines `MT-HALF-SISTER-POWER-SOURCE`: Enid remains Jack’s **enforced conduit** after Morgana is taken, consistent with shared maternal line / institutional extraction. |
| `MT-BOOK2-ILLUSORY-VICE-SEED` | `accepted` | Book 2 seed: a charged **illusory** relic made a **brothel** look fine while it was a **drug den**; when magic is plentiful, **Magicborn** (many, with duties) **police** misuse and **limit growth of the Tree of Tarro**. | Optional future volume; not Act I obligation. |
| `MT-MORDRED-MAGIC-SURGE-COST` | `accepted` | When **global magic returns**, Mordred’s power **spikes**; include a beat where he **struggles to control** what he does and **is hurt** by the overflow. | Act II/III bridge; contrasts quiet early casting. |
| `MT-MORGANA-MEMORY-SLEEP` | `accepted` | Morgana has **no meaningful gap** between sleep and labor — the transition reads **almost instant** to her. | Ch1–3 interiority. |
| `MT-MORGANA-PRIDE-LINE` | `accepted` | The thing she will still die before surrendering is **her pride**. | Captivity choices. |
| `MT-MORGANA-COUNCIL-COLLUSION` | `accepted` | She **would** collaborate with the council to recover Mordred; in practice it is **also impossible** — instead they **use her** to breed a **new child with Owain** (Enid). | Enid origin + moral stain. |
| `MT-MORDRED-INFANT-ACT-II-OPEN` | `accepted` | At the **House of Small Mercies** handoff, Mordred is still an **infant**. **Midas / gold** is an **accident** of touch while he is a baby. | Act II opener age-locks; amulet beat stays dangerous. |
| `MT-MORDRED-QUIET-CASTER-BARK` | `accepted` | Mordred is **quiet**, not nonverbal: he **practices alone**, is **gifted**, and speaks **minimal / strange syllables** to cast — often sounding like a **private language**. **Bark from the Tree of Tarro**, worn as a band, **blocks his spoken spells**; the orphanage **enforces** bark when they can. | Resolves `MT-MAGIC-SPEECH-VS-MORDRED-SILENCE` for draft; ties to Act III bark removal. |
| `MT-HOUSE-SMALL-MERCIES-CHURCH` | `accepted` | The House is a **church of the pantheon**, founded by **immigrants** who came to **excavate** the lair, **failed** to secure easy wealth, and stayed to build a **congregation** that worships **power and stability** attributed to the old gods. They are **not** in the business of taking children — **exception for Mordred** because his magic can make them **rich**. | Institution voice + hypocrisy. |
| `MT-HOUSE-PUBLIC-FACE` | `accepted` | Outsiders treat the place like an **adventurers’ guild with vestments**: you **check in** for permission to approach the lair. **Smell canon:** **beer and smoke** cling to the gate hall. | Set dressing + procedural texture. |
| `MT-SISTER-MERRIS` | `accepted` | **Sister Merris** — cellar and kitchen authority — is **not cartoon-evil** but **dangerous**: she believes **discipline sanctifies**, will **report** dissent, and mistakes **hunger** for **gratitude**. | Named “kind face / hard system” caretaker for Act II orphanage. |
| `MT-TANYA-OWAIN-HOUSEHOLD` | `accepted` | Owain **raises Enid** and **loves** her; **Jack** also **loves** her. **Morgana** is kept **apart**; Enid is raised to believe **Tanya** (Owain’s wife) is her mother and **fears and despises** Tanya. | Cast lock: **Tanya** on [state](/docs/books/mordreds-tale/planning/state). |
| `MT-GERAINT-TANYA-SON` | `accepted` | **Geraint** — Owain and **Tanya’s** son, raised beside **Enid**; the boy **blinded** by Enid’s unbanded bright spell ([`MT-ENID-BRIGHT-SPELL-ARC`](/docs/books/mordreds-tale/planning/decisions)). | Prose name lock; Act II household scenes. |
| `MT-ENID-POWER-AWARENESS` | `accepted` | Enid understands what her touch is for by about **age five**. She **sometimes refuses**; usual punishment is **one lashing** (few refusals because of that). | Character spine for compliance vs resistance. |
| `MT-ENID-BRIGHT-SPELL-ARC` | `accepted` | **Key incident:** Without her usual **bark band**, Enid tries to show **Geraint** (Tanya’s son) a **playful bright spell** — the light **blinds** him. Afterward she is **prisoner-like**: escort, restrictions, guards. Tanya later **isolates** her, claims **Geraint** will not speak to her until she **repents**, and offers **forgiveness** if she takes **twenty lashings** — then attempts to **blind** Enid after the twentieth; **Owain stops** Tanya and removes her. **Enid witnesses Tanya’s hanging** for this. | Act II scenes to draft; horror + Owain’s “hero” fracture. |
| `MT-GWYNETH-PATERNITY-SUSPECT` | `accepted` | Gwyneth **suspects** Jack is her father before the carriage reveal lands. | Act III interiority. |
| `MT-GWYNETH-BATTLE-WANT` | `accepted` | On the battlefield she wants from Mordred **who he is** and **how to do what he does** — kinship of power, not only survival. | Duel subtext. |
| `MT-HALLINGHORN` | `accepted` | Southern neighbor kingdom: **Hallinghorn** — culture reads **festive** even at war. War motive: **resources** vs Tarro’s **lair gate**; southern forces fight a **losing** but stubborn campaign. **Battlefield texture:** **meadow** country. | Act III geography. |
| `MT-LAW-SEALED-TONGUE` | `accepted` | Imperial–church statute (working title **Sealed Tongue**): speaking a **Fallen power’s true name** within **consecrated ground** of a **licensed chapel** without a **seal-bearer** witness is **forfeiture of ritual rights** and **automatic excommunication from delver registry** — crown may seize relics carried at arrest. (Wording can shift in prose; beat is **controlled speech around gods**.) | In-world legal texture; see Magicborn statute excerpt doc. |
| `MT-PRE-MAGICBORN-SLUR` | `accepted` | Before “Magicborn” exists as a named people, unchecked casters are folk-called **devils**. | Street language. |
| `MT-PUBLIC-LORE-SPOILERS` | `accepted` | **Magicborn** section docs may be **fully spoilery** relative to the unpublished book when marked for author/reader tooling. | Dossiers + in-world files can carry endgame facts. |
| `MT-ACT-II-THEIR-LIVES` | `accepted` | Act II is mainly about **Mordred and his half-sister** growing up and their experiences (orphanage + empire; castle meeting at 15/14; Merlin found when she is ~14). | Dual-thread structure; half-sister POV and role as power source. |
| `MT-ACT-III-CONFLICT` | `accepted` | Act III fast-forwards to the conflict. Mordred and Enid **remeet** on **opposite sides** (Mordred reluctantly helping friends; Enid and **Gwyneth** with Jack’s kingdom). Remeet on a **meadow battlefield** in the **Hallinghorn** war (`MT-HALLINGHORN`). | Act III opens in the conflict era; the remeet is a key beat. |
