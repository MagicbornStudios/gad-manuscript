# Statute of Dungeon Relic Custody

*Promulgated under joint seal of the Crown Chamber and the Magicborn Host — governing sorties into the **Tarro dungeon** (common name: the **regrown root** complex). Posted at every barrack mess and at the armory wicket. Fair copy for enlisted cadre.*

---

## Article I — Purpose

Relics recovered from the dungeon are **crown property** in potential. Until catalogued, they are **hazard** in certainty. This statute binds every Magicborn who descends to **named custody**, **timed return**, and **accountable silence**.

## Article II — Issue at the armory

1. No Magicborn shall enter the descent way **without** an armory issue slip.
2. Issue shall comprise **only** the relics and talismans listed on the slip, each struck with **bark-ink** sigil and the quartermaster's counter-mark.
3. The slip is **paired** to the soldier's roster name and sortie window. **Substitution** of relics between soldiers is **forbidden** unless the armory records a written exchange in the same hour.

## Article III — Carriage in the lair

1. Relics not **physically** on the soldier's person or in a **sealed Host pack** assigned to that soldier are deemed **stray**.
2. Stray relics discovered on a soldier's route may be **confiscated** and charged as **theft from the crown's future inventory**, regardless of intent.

## Article IV — Return and reconciliation

1. Within **one bell** of crossing the ward-line to the surface, the soldier shall **return every issued item** to the armory wicket for **reconciliation**.
2. The quartermaster shall strike each returned line and **feed** the bark-ink mark until it **closes**. An open mark at roster close is a **fault**.
3. If a relic is **lost**, **destroyed**, or **left below**, the soldier shall declare fault **before** the mark is forced closed. A false declaration is **perjury against the Host**.

## Article V — Penalty

1. **Fault** without timely declaration — **death**, carried out under Host law, without appeal to common kinship courts.
2. **Perjury** — **death**, and **forfeiture** of name on the roster of mercy (no public scaffold kinship; the Host does not share its dead with ordinary family law).
3. Officers may commute **only** line-of-duty loss **if** two witnesses and a **bark-auditor** certify the relic was **consumed** by dungeon hazard in their sight — commutation is **not** amnesty; it is **reclassification** to crown loss.

## Article VI — Plain sense clause

The dungeon eats **memory** and **metal** alike. The armory exists so the kingdom knows **what came back**. If you would keep a trinket, **desert** honestly — do not **lie** to the tally, or the tally will **lie** you into the ground.
