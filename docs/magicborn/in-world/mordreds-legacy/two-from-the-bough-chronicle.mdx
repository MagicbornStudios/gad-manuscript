# Two from the Bough

*From the **Annals of the Second Seating**, choir copy — fair rendering.*

---

They say the king’s first mistake was joy.

Magic had returned to the air like weather, and Tarro — still bleeding from the war that put him on the bench — believed it could be **shared**. The church signed in ink. The southern captains signed in salt. The sister and her daughter signed with their eyes still full of ash from the meadow. The crown thought **coalition** meant **safe**.

Then the **Tree** remembered it was not a metaphor.

It went up faster than scaffolding. Faster than prayer. The sky tore like cheap cloth, and **two** shapes descended the trunk as if it were a road built for feet the size of gatehouses. Not gods. **Giants** — bone and hunger and the stupid strength of things that do not negotiate.

They stepped on **streets**. The streets **stopped** being streets.

What followed was not a battle in the songs. It was **work**: the king casting until his voice **bled**; the sister stripping **bark** from her own skin to **speak** clearly; the daughter learning that **hate** and **need** can aim the same spear; the southern friends holding lines that kept breaking; the church ringing bells that sounded like **orders** instead of mercy.

When the second giant fell, Tarro was mostly **rubble** with a throne in the middle.

They **cut** the Tree. They **burned** the roots they could find. They wrote **laws** that pretended wood obeys ink.

The stump **grew** anyway.

That is why your son trains in **armor** before he learns to dance. That is why the **dungeon** has a roster. That is why other kingdoms brag of their little holes in the ground — and fall silent when Tarro is named.

Two came down.

Everyone living paid the toll.
