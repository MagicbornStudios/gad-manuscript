# Cider Rite — fragment

*In-world folk verse (fiction). Southern **Hallinghorn** voice; Act III war texture.*

---

We tie the ribbon on the spear —  
not for pretty, **for seeing clear**:  
the gate that eats the meadow’s year.

We drink the cider **sweet and brown**,  
we laugh the siege-line **upside down**,  
we kiss the ones who **might** drown.

If Tarro’s bells ring **charge** and **throat**,  
we answer **smoke** with **smoke** —  
and send the south a **living note**.

---

*Companion tone to southern allies **Rhys Wyn** and **Aldara of Brindleford** in Tale manuscript scaffolds.*
