# First Article of the Sealed Tongue (excerpt)

*Archival copy, crown chancery — fair rendering of the church’s binding clause as preached at licensed chapels.*

---

**Article the First — Of Names That Still Burn**

Let it be known to delvers, merchants, novices, and the merely curious: the stone that drinks prayer in a **licensed chapel** of the old pantheon is not neutral ground. It remembers the knee. It remembers the flatterer. It remembers the syllables that once opened doors without knocking.

Therefore, within the hearing of such consecrated stone, no person shall **speak the true name** of a **Fallen power** — neither in boast, nor in curse, nor in scholarly pride — unless a **Seal-Bearer of Tarro** stands witness with **sigil uncovered** and **registry mark** shown.

**Penalty:** forfeiture of **ritual rights** in all chapels under the joint seal; **striking** from the **delver registry**; **surrender** of relics carried at the moment of arrest, for examination by crown and choir together.

**Intent:** that the hungry dead not be **called home** by accident; that the black market not hide behind scholarship; that the **Tree** keep its thresholds **counted**.

*So sealed this year of our continued vigil under Tarro.*
