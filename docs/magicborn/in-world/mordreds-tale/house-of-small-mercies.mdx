# The House of Small Mercies

*Archival dossier — coalition reader's digest (fiction). Primary shelf: **Mordred's Tale**.*

No one storms the chamber with a sword. They arrive with seals, nursemaids, repeated phrases, and the confidence of people who believe paperwork makes harm smaller. The House of Small Mercies enters the story not as a shelter in the moral sense, but as the clean-faced machinery that absorbs a child once **Tarro** decides his mother is useful elsewhere — and the building **smells** of **beer and smoke** at the gate, because mercy here shares a wall with **trade**.

Below, a **ledger voice** and a **penitent voice** (in-world documents; **spoilery**) expand what the dossier table cannot hold.

## Known Facts

| Axis | Canon Note |
| --- | --- |
| `entry mode` | Mordred is taken by procedure rather than open force — an **infant** at separation. |
| `founding` | **Immigrant** faithful built the House after a **failed lair excavation**; they **worship the pantheon’s memory** as **power and stability**, not as neighbors who owned their grandparents. |
| `exception` | The order does **not** habitually take children; **Mordred** is an **exception** because his **well** can make the church **rich**. |
| `public myth` | Outsiders treat the gate like an **adventurers’ guild in vestments** — you **register** before you **descend**. |
| `moral language` | The institution is justified through phrases like "best for the child," which soften coercion without removing it. |
| `symbol` | The transport wagon bears a house with open hands, a visual language of mercy attached to control. |
| `transition object` | A cheap clay amulet is tied onto the child as part of the handoff, with Owain performing the knot himself. |
| `bark discipline` | Strips of **bark from the Tree of Tarro**, worn against the skin, **damp spoken spells**; staff **enforce** bands on the boy when they can. |
| `personnel` | **Sister Merris** (cellar, kitchen) — **dangerous** without being a caricature of evil: she believes **hunger teaches**, **discipline sanctifies**, and will **report** a child’s softness the way another nun might report theft. |
| `political role` | The House functions as a **church-of-pantheon** node inside **Tarro’s** wider power structure — aligned with crown **lair gate** logic. |
| `narrative role` | It marks the legal and emotional end of Morgana's remaining freedom and the beginning of Mordred's orphanage route. |
