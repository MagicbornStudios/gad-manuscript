# Lament of Serefall

*In-world letter-poem (fiction), attributed to **Mira Voss**, court poet of Serefall.*

---

To my sister in the lower orchard,

They asked me to write a wedding hymn for the king's second forge-night, when the copied bowl was set in the cellar and priests from three houses promised it would hold like a true lair.  
I wrote instead:

> A copied throat still wants a god.  
> A painted sun still dies at dusk.  
> If you pour blood where roots are absent,  
> the stone will drink the wrong first.

At midnight the bowl took light. At dawn it took walls.  
By noon the palace doors were inward-facing only.

Men in silk called it "instability." Mothers called it what it was: answer-fire.

If this reaches you, leave Serefall before the third bell.

— **Mira**

---

*Archivist note:* Serefall appears in later southern songs as one of the first kingdoms destroyed after attempting to engineer a pantheon-grade well from ritual copywork and forged relic channels.
