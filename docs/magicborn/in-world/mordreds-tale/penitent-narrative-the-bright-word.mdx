# Penitent Narrative — The Bright Word

*Anonymous fair copy, filed with a church that would rather own guilt than forgive it.*

---

I knew by **five** what my skin was for. Not love. **Currency.** They did not have to draw a picture. The relics in my father’s house **cooled** faster when I was made to stand in certain rooms. I learned to be **useful** before I learned to be **kind**.

I was not often brave. Disobedience bought a **single lash** — clean, counted, unforgettable. I chose compliance the way animals choose the fence they cannot jump.

Then I was **stupid** with wanting to be a child.

I slipped my **band** — the rough weave they said was **blessing**, bark innermost against the pulse — because I wanted to show **Geraint**, **Tanya’s** son, the boy I was raised beside, a **trick**. Playful words. A joke between children. *It will be bright,* I told him. *Be ready.*

The light I made was not a candle. It was **judgment** with a sense of humor. When the whiteness left, his eyes did not.

After that I was **escorted** everywhere. Doors had keys. Hallways had feet that were not mine. I became a daughter of **procedure**.

She came to me when I was alone — the woman who was not my mother but wore the house like a crown. She said my brother would not speak to me until I **repented**. She said **Tanya** could intercede if I would accept **twenty** in honesty.

I took the twenty. I counted them because counting is how you survive what you cannot stop.

On the last stroke I fell, and still she wanted more than my back. She wanted my **sight**. Father’s hands stopped her before the knife — or thumb — or whatever mercy’s opposite is called in a kitchen at night.

I watched her **hang** later, in **Tarro**’s square. The crowd was **festive** in the wrong way — **beer** and **smoke** and the same bright noise people make when cruelty wears a legal seal. I did not cheer. I did not close my eyes. I **owed** myself that witness.

They will call what I am a **devil** when the world fills with magic again and needs a word for the uncontained.

I call myself **Enid**.
