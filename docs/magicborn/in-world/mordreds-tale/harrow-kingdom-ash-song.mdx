# Ash Song of Harrow

*In-world road verse (fiction), collected under the name **Tern Gall**.*

---

Harrow built a second mouth  
under the mint and prayer-hall.  
Copper saints and antler crowns,  
seven judges, one red bowl.

"Make us what the old gods made."  
So the king told rootless priests.  
They wired children to their rings,  
called the shaking "harvest heat."

Night came white and left no roofs.  
Morning found the river glassed.  
All our banners learned one color:  
ash that does not ask.

---
