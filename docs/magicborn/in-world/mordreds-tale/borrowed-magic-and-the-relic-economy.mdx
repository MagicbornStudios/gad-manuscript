# Borrowed Magic and the Relic Economy

*Archival dossier — coalition reader's digest (fiction). Primary shelf: **Mordred's Tale**.*

After the Fall, relics do not stop mattering when they stop working. They become hunger made metal: goblets, crowns, rings, and pendants sold for what they once did and what buyers pray they might do again. The changed world runs on memory first, charge second — because **ambient magic is gone** for a long age, and what remains is **borrowed**: a glow that **runs down** until someone feeds it again. In the **Kingdom of Tarro**, Jack learns to move that hunger quietly: a sleeping goddess, then a mother holding a child who is himself a **well**, then other bodies the crown comes to treat as inventory. The economy of hope becomes an economy of controlled proof.

## Known Facts

| Axis | Canon Note |
| --- | --- |
| `post-Fall baseline` | Most of the old pantheon is **gone**; **Merlin** sleeps in the tree’s core, **Morgana** walks the world. The old free-flowing weave **failed**; people still **remember** magic like weather. |
| `ownership` | Law does not forbid relics to common hands; **black markets** and patronage move the charged ones fastest. |
| `dead-relic market` | Even inert relics still command value because people buy legend, status, and possibility. |
| `surface economy` | Scavengers profit from shallow lair runs, broken relics, and the stories attached to them. |
| `temporary charge` | Charge is **time-based** on the object. **Sleeping** Morgana (Jack’s early method); **Morgana holding Mordred** (well conducted through her); later **Enid** as enforced conduit — each a different moral temperature, same hunger. |
| `visual tell` | **Some** charged relics show their state to the trained eye; **not all**. |
| `city proof` | By the time Morgana reaches Tarro’s settlement, charged items and infrastructure prove someone solved **part** of the recharge problem without restoring the open sky. |
| `political consequence` | Borrowed charge is enough for **Tarro** to broker **favors, position, and fear** until Merlin wakes the old weave. |
