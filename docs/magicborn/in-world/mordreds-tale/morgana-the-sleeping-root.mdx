# Morgana, the Sleeping Root

*Archival dossier — coalition reader's digest (fiction). Primary shelf: **Mordred's Tale**.*

She wakes to labor in a chamber that still looks like hers, though the warmth has gone wrong. Later, in another strand of time, she is found asleep by a desperate man who learns that relics wake when skin touches skin. In the changed world, she carries her son through a city built on that theft and understands, before anyone says it plainly, that somebody has already learned how to live off her body.

## Known Facts

| `old-world status` | She was born **mortal**, a **kingdom’s gift** to **Merlin** — **immortality** forged for **his pleasure** and for **political persistence**. Her kingdom **fell** in the chaos after the pantheon broke; **refugees** scattered to neighbors. She and **Merlin** are what **remains** of that tier in the tale; the rest are **gone**. |
| `body history` | She had sacrificed her ability to conceive long before waking in labor with Mordred — and she **knows why** this pregnancy broke that vow. |
| `sleep-memory` | The sleep before labor feels **almost instant** to her; there is **no** useful corridor of missing years to cling to. |
| `lair relationship` | She knows the lair as home and moves through chambers that once held relics, bones, and overgrown remains of divine order. |
| `child bond` | While Mordred is in her arms, she feels whole and strong, and **his** life answers relic-hunger through her; when separated, that strength **diminishes**. |
| `pride` | The line she will not trade away, even for her life, is **pride**. |
| `council impasse` | She would **collaborate** to buy Mordred back if honor allowed; the settlement instead **uses** her body to make **another child** with Owain (**Enid**). |
| `sleeping state` | In Jack's past timeline, she sleeps under an enforced stillness so deep that direct contact with her body temporarily recharges dead relics. |
| `political value` | In the changed settlement, her knowledge, bloodline, and body are treated as exploitable assets rather than as a person's life. |
