# Jack of Taro and the Logic of Worth

*Archival dossier — coalition reader's digest (fiction). Primary shelf: **Mordred's Tale**.*

Before he becomes a patron with cold eyes, Jack is a man his own mother calls worth nothing. He learns that hope sells, that relics still command coin even when they are dead, and that a body in the right room can turn dead metal warm again. He **escapes** the lair’s depths and begins a rhythm: **months** on the surface building **Tarro**, **nights** below **recharging** and **stealing** what he can — until the sleeping woman is **simply gone**, and his life narrows into a hunt through **connections** until the net closes. His morality becomes a method: keep the wells hidden, move the charge quietly, buy obedience before anyone can call it a kingdom.
