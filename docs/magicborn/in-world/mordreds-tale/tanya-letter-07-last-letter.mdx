# Letter VII — Last letter

Mother,

This will be the last letter you receive from me with my own hand. I know that the way a trapped animal knows the wood has closed — not because a priest pronounced sentence, not because a magistrate read my name, but because I crossed a line I swore to myself I would never cross. I hurt a child with intent. I tried to pay pain back in the coin I thought the world owed me.

I lashed her until my arm ached and the strap left marks I will see when I close my own eyes.

I wanted her to hurt the way Geraint hurts — not only in skin, in *everything*. I wanted her to understand blindness as a debt. I took her face in my hands the way a mother might hold a daughter to comfort — only my thumbs were not kind. I pressed until she cried out. I tried to take her eyes. I failed.

Someone stopped me — Owain, a servant, I cannot say clearly; the moment is a blur of noise and hands pulling me back — or she was stronger than a girl should be, or something in the air *pushed* at me the way it does around her when she is afraid. Or God, if He still looks at me, spared me the full completion of my sin and left me only with the part I cannot undo: the wanting.

I do not ask you to forgive me. Forgiveness from far away would feel like another pretty lie. I ask you to remember that I was not always this — that once I crossed the border for love, not for hatred; that once I believed Owain when he said we would build a life.

If you hear of me after, do not send flowers. Do not send silences dressed as tact. Send truth, even if it is ugly. Tell my brothers, if they ask, that their sister learned what Tarro does to soft women and chose hardness instead.

Tell Father I am sorry for the worry this will bring. Tell him anyway. He would rather know than guess.

I love you. That is the last clean sentence I have.

**Tanya**
