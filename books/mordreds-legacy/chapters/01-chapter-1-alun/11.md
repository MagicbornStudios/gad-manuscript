The square had been built for this, whether anyone admitted it or not: steps rising on three sides, a platform, a beam, ropes hung with obscene patience.

A crowd was not invited. It was assembled.

Alun stood where he was told to stand—close enough to see, far enough to be safe from the thrashing. Someone held his elbow. He did not look to see who.

His parents mounted the steps.

His mother looked for him in the faces. When she found him, her mouth shaped *live*—not sound, just shape.

His father stared straight ahead.

Kael's voice cut through murmurs like a blade through cloth. He did not shout. He did not need to.

"Under statute," he said, "concealment of unlicensed Magicborn power within a household is treason against the crown's peace. Kin who shelter and lie forfeit the protections of ordinary family law."

Alun did not understand every word.

He understood *forfeit.*

He understood *nooses.*

When the traps dropped, the crowd made a noise—one body flinching.

Alun's knees buckled.

The world narrowed to rope, to stillness, to the brutal fact that stories about rivers listening had never mentioned this part.

Kael looked down at him, face unchanged.

"The kingdom keeps order," he said.

Alun could not answer.

There was no answer that would bring breath back.
