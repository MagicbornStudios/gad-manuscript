At a relay house, a **choir elder** climbed in — old hands, soft voice, eyes like ink wells.

The carriage became crowded with **faith** and **paper**.

The elder spoke of **registry**, of **duty**, of the Tree's danger once it remembered it could **grow**.

Mordred listened.

He did not trust the church.

He also knew he could not rule Tarro without it — not yet, not with the world still learning what free magic did to hungry people.

Enid's jaw tightened.

Gwyneth watched the elder the way a soldier watches a new weapon.

Rhys, outside, spat into the dust — not at God, at **bureaucracy**.
