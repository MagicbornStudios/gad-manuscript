The carriage smelled like **wax** and **rain** on leather.

**Enid** sat across from Mordred with her hands folded — discipline learned in a house that had used love as leverage.

**Gwyneth** sat beside her mother and watched Mordred like he was a door she could open if she found the right word.

Outside, **Jack's** escort rode close enough to hear breathing.

Inside, nobody spoke first.

Then Enid said, "She wants to know how you do it."

Mordred looked at Gwyneth.

Gwyneth nodded once — **hunger**, not flirtation.

"Kinship," she said. "Or theft. I can't tell yet."

Mordred's throat tightened.

He still did not know whose blood had made him.

He knew whose blood had made **her** — and he did not say it, because some truths arrive like knives.
