"I hate him," Enid said — **Jack** — plain as bread.

Mordred believed her.

He hated Jack too, for different reasons, different shapes.

Gwyneth's mouth twisted. "He's useful."

"So is a rope," Enid answered.

The carriage rocked.

Mordred listened to two generations of women negotiate **hatred** and **need** — the coalition emotion, the one that built thrones when love wouldn't.

He thought of Morgana in a vault.

He thought of Tanya on a rope.

He thought: *I will not build this on silence.*

Then he wondered if silence was all any king ever had.
