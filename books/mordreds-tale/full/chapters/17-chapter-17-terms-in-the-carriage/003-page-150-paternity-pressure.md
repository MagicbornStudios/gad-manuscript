Gwyneth leaned forward.

"If you're going to rule," she said, "you need **lines** people can read."

Mordred heard **paternity** underneath — not his own mystery, **hers**.

Enid's eyes flicked to her daughter — warning, love, **fear**.

The carriage air tightened.

"You don't owe anyone your story," Enid said — to Gwyneth, to Mordred, to herself.

Gwyneth laughed once, brittle. "I owe myself **truth**."

Mordred looked at the girl and saw **Jack** in her jaw again — and still did not know enough to name it.

The cabin felt too small for three truths and four souls.
