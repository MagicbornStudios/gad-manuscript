When the benches emptied, the hall felt bigger — as if hope took space.

Mordred stood on the steps and looked at **Tarro** — broken in places, still **there**.

Gwyneth caught up to him.

"You still won't tell me," she said — half joke, half ache — "how you **speak**."

Mordred almost told her the truth: *I speak like someone who practiced in cellars.*

Instead he said, "Come to lessons."

She brightened — dangerous, beautiful.

Enid watched them both like a storm watch.

Jack, in his cage of titles, watched too — and said nothing.

Good.

Silence could be a mercy.

When it wasn't a weapon.
