They did not execute Jack in the hall.

They **caged** him in titles — **prisoner-patron**, a monstrous compromise — because Tarro still needed a face for old bargains, because murder would have made him martyr, because **Enid** still flinched at his name.

Jack smiled like a man who had planned for cages.

Mordred leaned forward.

"You don't own me," Mordred said.

Jack's eyes flickered — almost **father**, almost **nothing**.

"Nobody owns free air," Jack answered softly.

It sounded like wisdom.

It tasted like poison.
