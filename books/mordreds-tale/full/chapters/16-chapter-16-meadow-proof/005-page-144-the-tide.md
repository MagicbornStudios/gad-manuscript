When Mordred finally **turned** the fight, it looked like a tide — not a duel, a **weather system**.

Jack's line broke in places he hadn't intended.

Men ran — not from cowardice, from **physics**.

Mordred lifted his hand and felt the world **lean**.

He could destroy the army.

He stopped.

Not because mercy was pure.

Because Rhys had asked him once: *If you win, buy us a seat.*

Annihilation was not a seat.
