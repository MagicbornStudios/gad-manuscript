Enid stripped bark bands from her wrists — old habit, old suppression — and the air **touched** her skin like a lover.

She had **whirlwind** scars; Mordred had more.

They didn't apologize for their bodies.

They apologized with **spells**.

Gwyneth fired first — **fire** shaped like a question — and Mordred answered with **gravity**, bending the heat aside, sweat on his upper lip, splint arm screaming anyway.

The rings on his fingers — relic tools, borrowed power — clicked like dice.

He was stronger than he wanted to be.

That was the horror of it.
