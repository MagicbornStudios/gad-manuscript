Enid stripped her top half to free **bark** scars across her torso — not shame, **tactics**.

The crowd made a noise — ugly, human — and Mordred looked away because looking felt like stealing.

"Don't you dare pity me," she hissed.

"I don't," he said, and meant it.

Pity was for stories.

This was **work**.

She came at him again — **fire**, **wind**, **speech** — and he met her with **gravity** and **deflection** and the brutal truth of **overwhelming** ability.

The meadow learned who would stand when the smoke cleared.
