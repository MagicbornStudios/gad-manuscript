When Owain came back downstairs with keys and a lamp, Morgana was still on the cot.

She had straightened the relics into a line — not for him. For herself. As if order could defend what dignity Jack had tried to slap away.

Owain paused on the threshold like a man stepping into a church he didn’t believe in.

"I’m sorry," he said — stupid, true.

Morgana looked at him.

Owain flinched from that look harder than he’d flinched from blades.

"I’m not…" He swallowed. "I’m not going to pretend this is anything else."

Morgana’s lips parted.

For a heartbeat, Owain thought she might speak — curse, bargain, beg.

She didn’t.

She turned her face to the wall.

Owain exhaled, ashamed of his relief.

Outside, Jack waited in the corridor with a face like a ledger page — all numbers, no names.

"She doesn’t fight you," Owain said quietly.

Jack’s mouth twisted. "She doesn’t need to. Silence is a blade too."

He walked away.

Owain stayed a moment longer — heroism trying to find a door in a room with none.

Then he locked the cell.

The bolt sounded like a promise neither of them would keep.
