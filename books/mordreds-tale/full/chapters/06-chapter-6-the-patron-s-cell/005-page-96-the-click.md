It clicked the way a lock did when you finally understood you’d been pushing the wrong key all along.

**Pregnant.**

Not romance. Not mystic poetry. **Biology** — the one thing Jack trusted because it always cost someone else more than it cost him.

When she’d carried the child, the world had **opened** against her skin. When she’d held the child, the charge had **run** — not because Morgana loved anyone, not because she was grateful, not because she belonged to him.

Because the **boy** was a **well**.

And she had been the **cup**.

Jack stepped back as if the air near her had turned poison.

His hands shook.

He stared at the dead bracelet on her wrist and saw months of **lies** he’d told himself — that he’d **tamed** a goddess, that he’d **earned** a sleeping bride, that power was **his** because he’d been brave enough to touch what others feared.

The power had been **in** her only while she carried what he’d **stolen**.

Now the theft was elsewhere — behind choir seals and registry ink — and the woman on the cot was just…

**A woman.**

Beautiful still.

Dangerous still.

But **empty** of the one thing Tarro’s machine required.

Jack laughed once — a broken sound.

Morgana didn’t flinch.

Jack wiped his mouth. His knuckles hurt. He didn’t care.

"You should have told me," he whispered.

Morgana’s eyes said everything her mouth wouldn’t: *You never asked the right question.*

Jack turned to the door and hammered it with his fist.

Owain opened it fast — too fast, like he’d been listening and hated himself for it.

Jack’s voice came out flat.

"Find the boy," he said.

Owain’s face tightened. "The House—"

"I know what the House is." Jack’s eyes were bright with something worse than rage. "**Find** him. Bribe. Burn. I don’t care."

Owain swallowed. "If they won’t release him—"

Jack looked past him into the stone hall — into the future he could still buy if he moved quickly enough.

"Then we make another one," he said.

Owain went pale.

Jack held his gaze until the man understood there wasn’t going to be a heroic refusal — only the shape refusal took when it met money and fear.

"**You**," Jack said. "Her. Until it takes."

Morgana made a sound then — not words.

A breath like a door slamming somewhere far away.

Jack didn’t look back at her.

He couldn’t.

Not yet.

Not until he’d turned Owain into a father.
