Jack loaded her like a rack.

Bracelet on wrist. Ring on finger. Pendant chain across collarbone. Another ring — Maliphant’s taste, ugly and reliable — pressed to the hollow of her throat until the sigil left a red mark.

He counted seconds the way he’d counted them in the lair.

Ten.

Twenty.

Forty.

**Nothing.**

The relics remained dead — not the hopeful dead of the market, not the dead you could lie about. **Truly** dead. Inert. Mocking.

Morgana’s chest rose and fell. Her eyes tracked him when he moved — not frantic. **Steady.**

As if she were seeing a man strip himself naked without touching his clothes.

Jack’s voice rose without permission.

"Say it," he said. "Say **thank you**. Say you **love** me. Say we can have **everything**."

Silence.

He grabbed her chin — not gentle, not yet cruel — tilting her face toward the light.

"**Tell me.**"

Morgana’s jaw stayed shut.

Jack slapped her.

The sound was small. Obscene in how small it was — a petty sound for a petty man doing a king’s crime.

Morgana’s head turned with the blow. Color rose on her cheek.

She did not cry out.

She did not speak.

Jack’s breath came hard.

"**Tell me,**" he said again — louder, as if volume could replace truth.

He hit her again — palm, then knuckles, uglier, his body doing what his mind hadn’t decided yet.

Morgana rocked. Her hands lifted once — not to strike back, to **brace** — then stilled, as if she’d remembered that fighting him on his terms was another kind of surrender.

Jack caught her shoulder and shoved her back to the cot.

Relics slid. Metal clattered.

Still **no** charge.

Only his heartbeat, too fast, too loud, filling the room like proof of life — **his** life — the only thing answering him.

"**Tell me,**" he rasped.

Morgana looked at him through swelling skin.

Silent.
