Jack wrote the order himself — not to the council, not to the church. To Owain’s hand.

No seal needed. Fear sealed better than wax.

*You will attend the prisoner until conception. You will not speak of it as love. You will not speak of it as sin. You will speak of it as **work** — the same work that feeds your house, your wife, your name.*

Owain read it in the stable’s lantern light, horses shifting behind him like witnesses who wouldn’t testify.

Jack stood in the straw smell and watched him finish.

"If the boy surfaces," Jack said, "we pivot. If he doesn’t—"

"He won’t," Owain whispered. "Not if Merris has him."

Jack’s eyes glittered. "Then we **make** what we need."

Owain’s jaw tightened. "Tanya—"

"Will eat because you obey," Jack said. "Same as always."

Owain looked like a man realizing heroism was a costume he’d rented and the rental shop was closed.

He folded the paper until it was small enough to hide in his glove.

Jack turned away.

Above them, Tarro kept building its bright lie — fountains, markets, council halls — all of it resting on a truth Jack had only now learned to name.

Not Morgana.

Not gratitude.

A **child** you could **hold**.

And if you couldn’t hold the first one —

—you bred the second.

In the cell below, Morgana lay in the dark with relics cooling beside her like failed prayers.

She did not sleep.

She listened for footsteps.

And when Owain’s boots finally scraped the stone again — slower than before — she closed her eyes.

Not to rest.

To **endure**.
