He woke to **pain** that knew its address: **right arm** — wrong angle, wrong heat.

Mordred tried the healing syllables he’d heard in the House cellars, the ones that fixed **skin** and **bruise** and other polite hurts.

Flesh knitted.

Bone stayed **broken**.

He tried again, louder, desperate — and the magic **slid** off the break like water off wax, as if his body refused to let him **fully** fix himself.

Someone laughed in the distance — not cruel, **wild** — the world drunk on new air.

Mordred staggered up. The meadow tilted. He cradled his arm and realized, with a clarity that felt like adulthood, that he would need **another person** — **aid** — someone else’s hands, someone else’s voice, someone else’s permission.

Independence had been a story for a world without free magic.

That world was over.

Something **moved** in the sky — not weather, **someone** — a body **tumbling** through new air as if flight had to **remember** how lungs worked. She **righted** with a snap of will and **hung** there a moment, **wild**, **alone**.

Tarro behind her coughed smoke and **wrong** bells. From this distance the chaos looked like **weather** — until you knew what you were seeing: a kingdom **punished** by its own **souvenirs**.

She was **older** than the stories allowed. **Lines** framed her mouth; **thinness** clung to her wrists. Then, as if the world had **apologized** for the years it withheld power, **health** rushed back — **color**, **strength**, a **youthfulness** that did not quite **erase** the wait. You could still read the **gap** in her skin.

She flew like a person **leaving** a name behind.

Mordred watched her track toward the open country and understood, with the clean selfishness of pain, that he was not her business.

She was going to **pass** him.

He had nothing left but **noise**. He raised his good hand and spoke the **shape** that had once been only **infant** reflex — the **well** syllables, the pull that had lived in his first months before words — **small**, **honest**, **exactly** the wrong thing to waste on a stranger.

The woman **stopped** as if the air had **caught** her throat.

Her eyes found him — and **recognized** something that had **nothing** to do with faces.

She **dove**. The ground **lurched**. Her arms slid under his shoulders and knees — a cradle built for **war** — and he smelled **stone** and **grief** and **power** like a storm **coming home**.

"You **fool**," she said — and the word tried to be **anger** and landed as something **rawer**.

Then they were **air** again, **south** and **south**, Tarro a **wound** behind them, the **Hallinghorn** hills a line she **read** in the wind as if she had been waiting only for **this** direction.

He still did not know her name was **Morgana**.

He only knew: **aid** had **found** him, and it flew.
