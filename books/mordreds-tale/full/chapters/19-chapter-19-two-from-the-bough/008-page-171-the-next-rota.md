**Tarro** slept uneasy — the sleep of a kingdom that had chosen **bench** over blade for its next chapter.

**Mordred** slept with his splint off at last, arm healed enough to be **his** again — bone set by hands not his own, a lesson in it.

**Enid** dreamed of rope and fire — old fears — and woke with the window open.

**Gwyneth** dreamed of spells she did not yet know, and woke hungry.

In the vault below, **Morgana** breathed — one quiet fact still waiting for its sentence.

Above, the **Tree** whispered leaves against stone.

The first age of the **bench** closed on **law**, **coalition**, and the honest promise of more work.

What came next would begin where duty always begins — in the **dungeon** line, in the **stump**, in the stories a nation makes its children read so they can survive what comes down from the sky.
