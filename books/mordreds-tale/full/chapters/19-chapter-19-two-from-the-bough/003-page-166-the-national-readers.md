Afterward, a scribe pressed a fresh booklet into Mordred's good hand — cheap cover, expensive words.

**National Reader — Series III: Duty Under Free Air**

Inside: rotas, bark law where it still applied, registry oaths — and a appendix title that made him pause:

*Giants of the Bough — required familiarity for all Magicborn in crown service.*

Not a story for tonight.

A **curriculum**.

A promise that the kingdom would **teach** its catastrophes so soldiers didn't have to learn them only in blood.

Mordred thought of **Legacy** — the stump era, the dungeon — and understood why the church wanted reading to be part of duty.

Knowledge was another kind of chain.

Sometimes chains kept streets from becoming graves.
