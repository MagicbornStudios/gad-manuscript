Late, Mordred walked alone to the wall where he used to feel small on purpose.

Small still fit.

So did **power**.

He practiced **gravity** in tiny pulls — pebbles, leaves — the same discipline as campfires, the same boy-habit.

The sky was full of new stars — or old stars seen through new air.

Somewhere, a teacher opened a **national reader** and began a lesson about **giants** — history as warning, law as spine.

Somewhere else, a dungeon rota formed — names, shifts, **duty**.

The story continued — not as one book, but as many **documents** trying to keep a kingdom from eating itself.

Mordred was one of them now.

He signed his name in the air with a syllable — small, controlled — and felt the world **answer**.

Not as surge.

As **work**.
