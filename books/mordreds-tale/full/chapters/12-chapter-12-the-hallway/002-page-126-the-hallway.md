They met where stone turned — a bend that made guards overlap for a breath.

**Enid** wore silk like armor. Her eyes still held rope-light; she smiled the way you smile when you’ve learned what smiles cost.

"You shouldn’t be here," she said.

Mordred heard **marriage** underneath the sentence — Hallinghorn, Halric, not love, **placement**.

Footsteps behind him: her escort, his minders, the kingdom’s polite teeth.

Conflict tightened like a wire.

Mordred did not want to hurt her. He also did not want to be **placed**.

He reached for the private syllables that lived behind his teeth — the gravity cant that wasn’t quite prayer — and **pulled** weight out of the air. His body lifted a finger’s width. The floor’s grip loosened.

Enid’s guard stumbled as if the world had tilted.

Mordred **slid** — not ran, not fought — along the seam of confusion, a boy using borrowed physics like a thief using shadow.

Down a side stair. Toward the smell of damp and old smoke.

Toward the place Jack had been shouting about all week: **deeper**.
