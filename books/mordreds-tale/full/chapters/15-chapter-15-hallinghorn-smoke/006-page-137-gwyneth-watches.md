**Gwyneth** studied Mordred the way a smith studies metal — not with romance, with **interest**.

She wanted what he could do.

She wanted **how**.

When their eyes met across the smoke, Mordred felt a question land in his ribs like an arrow wrapped in silk: *Who are you to her? To **me**?*

He did not have an answer that wouldn't become a lie later.

Enid said something to her daughter — sharp, protective — and Gwyneth looked away.

Family, Mordred thought, was also a kind of war.
