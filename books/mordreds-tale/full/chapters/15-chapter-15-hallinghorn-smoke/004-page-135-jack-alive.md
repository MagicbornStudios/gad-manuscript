Jack watched from a rise in his own colors — **alive**, then, not puppet, not ghost — a man who had bet on control and lost the bet without losing the crown.

His smile, even at distance, looked like a wound that enjoyed bleeding.

Mordred felt the old child-fear — **grille**, **silence**, **almost-father** — and crushed it under newer anger.

Jack was not his father in any story Mordred believed.

Jack was the man who had built stairs into other people's throats.

If the war ended with Jack still breathing, it would not be because Mordred lacked power.

It would be because power had learned **terms**.
