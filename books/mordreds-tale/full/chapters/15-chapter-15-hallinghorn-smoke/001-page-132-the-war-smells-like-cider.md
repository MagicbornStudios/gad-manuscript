**Hallinghorn** had made a festival of survival — ribbons on spears, songs in the siege lines, **cider** sweet enough to cut smoke. Mordred had come south by **ways** the map did not **teach**; his **right** arm still carried the **night** the world **remembered** how to **fly**.

**Captain Rhys Wyn** leaned over a mud map and pointed at Tarro’s gate-economy like it was a vein he intended to open.

Beside him, **Aldara of Brindleford** — envoy, healer, **knife** at her belt dressed as courtesy — watched the southern boys pretend they weren’t afraid.

Mordred sat with them because friendship had become strategy.

Jack still lived — **king** in title if not in mercy — and his banners flew over **meadow** country like a dare.

Enid rode on the other side now: enemy colors, **Gwyneth** at her flank, both of them shining with new magic like girls turned weapons.

The war was not clean.

It was **honest**.
