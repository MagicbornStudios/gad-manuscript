Night fell. Fires drew circles. Someone played a string badly on purpose — Hallinghorn humor: if you can't be good, be **loud**.

Mordred sat outside the noise and practiced **gravity** in tiny safe pulls, pebbles lifting and settling, discipline like a prayer.

Tomorrow, the meadow would ask for more than pebbles.

Tomorrow, **Enid** would come down off her horse for **proof**.

Rhys found him and sat without asking permission.

"If you win," Rhys said, "you buy us a seat."

"If I lose," Mordred answered, "you lose a friend."

Rhys grinned. "Then don't lose."

Simple words.

War loved them.
