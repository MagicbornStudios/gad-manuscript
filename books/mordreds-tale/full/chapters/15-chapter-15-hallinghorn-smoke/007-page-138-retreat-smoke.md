The first clash did not decide anything.

It only taught the grass how to drink.

They pulled back under cover while the meadow hissed with spent spells — **wind**, **fire**, half-formed things that looked like childhood nightmares with teeth.

Rhys clapped Mordred on the good shoulder. "Still breathing. Good."

Mordred's broken arm throbbed anyway — a reminder that **power** and **body** were still two different arguments.

Aldara pressed a wet cloth to his forehead like he was any soldier.

"You fight like you're trying not to become him," she said quietly.

Mordred didn't ask who **him** meant.

He knew.
