"We don't need Tarro's throne," Rhys said. "We need Tarro's **throat**. The lair gate. The tree's mouth."

Mordred nodded, though the nod pulled at the splint on his arm — **set** by Aldara's hands, not his own spells. Humiliation and gratitude had braided tight.

Aldara tapped the map. "If we win the grass, we lose the peace. If we win the **bench**, we buy years."

"Jack won't sit a bench," Mordred said.

"Then we break his chair," Rhys answered, grinning like a man who enjoyed impossible work.

Outside the tent, **smoke** rose — not Hallinghorn festival smoke. **War** smoke.

Mordred thought of Enid's face in the hallway, rope-light in her eyes.

He thought of **Tanya**'s neck.

He thought: *I can end this.*

He did not know yet whether ending would mean **mercy**.
