The House did not stop being loud after the gold.

Mordred learned that before he learned most words: bodies moving fast, doors speaking in their hinges, voices pitched to carry and then pitched higher when they remembered small ears.

The registry hall smelled like the gate — **beer**, **smoke**, ink — and something new underneath, a sweat-sour fear that made adults glance at him the way people glance at a blade left on a table.

Someone had written a fresh line beside his name.

He could not read it. He could feel it anyway — the way a dog feels weather — because the sisters tightened when they said it, because **Merris**’s mouth went thin, because the world had decided he was a kind of problem that required paperwork.

Outside, Tarro whispered about a **gilded man**.

Inside, they whispered a different word.

He did not know yet that it would stick to his skin.
