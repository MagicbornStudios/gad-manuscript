Later — hours, maybe a day; time was mush when you were small and punished — Mordred sat with porridge he ate because hunger won.

Merris watched him eat.

"You did well," she said, and the praise felt like a trap.

He looked up.

She softened, almost imperceptibly. "Some men are storms. You don’t walk into storms to prove you’re brave. You wait."

Mordred thought of the grille.

He thought of eyes like Jack’s — bright, hungry, furious — and the way they had looked at him as if he were a door that wouldn’t open.

He thought of the word *devil* and the word *son* and how neither had been spoken in that hallway.

Outside, Tarro kept selling relics and lies in the same breath.

Inside, a child finished his supper and learned, without being told, that **near** was not **yours**.

The House held him.

The registry named him.

And the man behind the iron had walked away without calling his name.
