*Devil.*

They did not always say it to his face. Often it was behind hands, behind doors, behind the pious pleasure of naming evil so you didn’t have to name a child.

*Sealed blessing*, someone said, and meant the opposite of blessing.

*Unclean touch.*

*Miracle* — spit like a curse.

Mordred sat on cold stone while a sister’s fingers checked the **bark** band at his wrist as if the wood could answer for him. The strip itched. It also *pressed*, a dull weight that made his tongue feel too big when he wanted to shape sounds that were not quite speech.

He had sounds.

He kept them small because small things survived.

When an elder passed with a relic chain clicking like a warning bell, Mordred watched the metal and felt heat rise behind his eyes — not anger. Something hungrier.

The elder looked at him and crossed himself.

Mordred did not know the gesture’s full meaning.

He learned the shape of fear anyway.
