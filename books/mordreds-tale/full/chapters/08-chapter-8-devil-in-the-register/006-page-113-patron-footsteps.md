Important feet sounded different on the stairs.

Mordred learned that too — the weight of boots that expected space to open, the pause before a door as if the whole House should rearrange itself around a name.

The sisters moved faster.

Merris’s voice dropped into its smoothest register — the one she used for donors.

Someone said **Jack** — not always the full name, sometimes *the patron*, sometimes a title wrapped like silk around a blade — and Mordred felt the air change.

He was not supposed to be near the front hall.

He was brought near anyway, washed, bark checked, hair tugged straight — handled like an object that had to look presentable without looking **wanted**.

Merris knelt again.

"If he asks," she said, "you say nothing fancy. You say *yes, sister*. You say *thank you*."

Mordred looked at her.

Her eyes were afraid too, in a way she would never admit.

That fear made him braver.

Not safe.

Braver.
