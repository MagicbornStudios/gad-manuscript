Through a high window, Tarro showed him sky in slices.

Through lower gossip, Tarro showed him teeth.

A market woman said a child had **touched** a dead relic and a man had become coin.

A delver said the patron’s dues were tightening — too many hungry relics, not enough charge — and the church was hiding something in the cellar.

A boy too young to know better pointed at the House gate and said, *That’s where they keep the little devil.*

Mordred listened from behind a crate while Merris pretended not to notice him listening.

Knowledge was dangerous.

So was being known.

That night he dreamed of gold skin and screaming — not as story, as **texture** — and woke with his hands hot until the bark band cooled them back into obedience.

He hated the band.

He clung to it anyway, because the world outside the band was worse.
