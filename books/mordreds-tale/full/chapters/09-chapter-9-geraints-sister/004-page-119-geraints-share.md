**Seasons later**, when **Geraint** was toddling and **Enid** had already learned which adults softened for her, the boy learned jealousy before he learned fairness.

Owain saw it in the way Geraint hovered when Jack’s gifts arrived — soft cloth, a silver rattle absurd for an infant, a nurse’s coin left on the table like an apology without words.

"For the little one," Jack always said.

Geraint asked once, voice small, "For me?"

Jack patted his head and answered with distraction — not cruelty, not kindness; the absent-minded pat of a man whose mind was on **power**.

Geraint’s face closed.

Owain lifted him up. "You’re my heir," he whispered, too loud to be only for the boy, loud enough for Tanya to hear across the room. "You’re the one I teach to ride. You’re the one who carries our name."

It was true.

It was also a bandage on a wound that would widen.

Tanya’s mouth twitched — not gratitude, something tired.

Enid watched the ceiling with dark eyes too attentive for a baby.

Owain wondered, with a chill he didn’t voice, what exactly Jack had put in her blood besides his story.
