Jack came without knocking — patron privilege — and the room shrank.

He took Enid as if he had a right to her weight, and the ease of it made Owain’s stomach twist. Not jealousy, exactly. Something uglier: recognition that Jack’s love and Jack’s use were braided tight.

"She’s strong," Jack murmured, testing a tiny fist with his thumb.

Tanya stood at the hearth with her shoulders squared like a soldier. No second child yet to hide behind her — only the truth of **Enid**, who was everyone’s favorite story and nobody’s clean daughter.

Jack’s gaze found the infant and did not pretend to divide its attention fairly.

Owain felt the insult on Tanya’s behalf before he felt it on his own.

"Patron," Tanya said, voice careful.

Jack looked up. "Tanya. You’re well?"

The question was courtesy theater. He didn’t wait for an answer.

He spoke to Owain about **charge**, about **metal**, about what the kingdom needed now that the first well was out of reach — language half shop, half church, all hunger.

Enid made a small sound.

Jack’s eyes softened in a way that made Owain want to leave the room.

Warmth could be wrong too.
