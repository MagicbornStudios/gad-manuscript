Owain lay awake between duty and love until they stopped feeling like different things.

Tanya’s back was a wall.

Geraint snuffled in sleep, small and ordinary and safe in the way boys were allowed to be safe.

In the cradle, Enid breathed — watched, wanted, **useful**.

Owain thought of Morgana’s eyes lifting once, finding his, offering nothing.

He thought of Jack’s voice: *You’ll do it again when I say.*

He thought of registry stamps and night rosters and the clean lie he’d told Tanya until the lie became a language she could repeat to neighbors without flinching.

Heroism, he realized, was not a sword.

It was a story you told so you could sleep.

He was running out of story.

Still, when Enid whimpered, he rose to check her band.

Still, when Geraint dreamed and kicked, he soothed him with a hand on his back.

Split hearts kept beating.

That was their tragedy.

That was their job.
