What happened next was not desire. It was procedure — and procedure can be worse, because it asks you to pretend you are not choosing.

Owain told himself: *orders*, *kingdom*, *relics*, *the child elsewhere*, *the Patron’s ledger*. He layered the **cover** underneath like padding — *escort*, *sealed tongue rules*, *registry won’t stamp without a body on the line* — the sentences he would have used if he’d been doing the job he pretended he was doing. The cruelty was that the sentences fit. Tarro had made virtue and procedure rhyme on purpose.

Morgana did not speak.

When she breathed, it was shallow, controlled, as if she had decided that even pain would not get a voice out of her.

Jack had called it a second well.

Owain understood the economics before he understood himself. That was the part that would rot him later.
