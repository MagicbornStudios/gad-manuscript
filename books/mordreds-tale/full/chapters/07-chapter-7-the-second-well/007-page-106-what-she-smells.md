"You smell like the gate," Tanya said. "Beer. Smoke. And something underneath — like a chapel lamp burned too long."

Owain almost laughed — wrong building, wrong lie — but laughter would have opened him.

He leaned on the cover until it bore weight: "Clerks had lamps up half the night. Registry’s still rewriting lines after the… rumors. Jack wanted eyes he trusts on the paperwork — you know how patrons are — and the chapel witness won’t sign without smoke in the dish."

Every word was a profession he had actually performed.

Every word was also a door he was closing.

Tanya nodded once, the way you nod when you decide not to start a war you can’t win at midnight.

She touched his wrist. Her fingers were cold.

Owain wanted to confess until he was clean. He wanted to be the kind of man confession would fix.

Instead he kissed her forehead like a husband.

The kiss tasted like ash in his mouth.

Somewhere under Tarro, a woman stayed silent in a cell, and the kingdom kept turning because cruelty had paperwork.
