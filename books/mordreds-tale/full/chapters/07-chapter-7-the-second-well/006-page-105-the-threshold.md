Home smelled like bread and cheap wine — the life he was supposed to be proud of.

Tanya was awake. She sat at the table with her hands around a cup she wasn’t drinking, heat gone from the clay, eyes red at the rims like she’d been pretending sleep.

Owain stopped on the threshold.

He knew she would ask where he had been.

He knew he would lie in pieces — truth enough to move her worry, not enough to end it.

"Patron priority on the roster," he said, the phrase coming out smooth because he had practiced it in his head the way he practiced **registry** answers. "Night convoy — charge-sensitive. They needed a second seal before the forms clear. You know how the House is."

Tanya looked at his hands.

Owain realized, too late, that he had missed a fleck under his nail.

She saw it anyway. Women always did when they loved you and were afraid of you.

She did not scream. That was worse.
