Owain told himself the worst was a single night.

Then Jack said *again*, and the chain of keys learned his hand.

The **relic-hunter** mask held because it was built to hold: Owain came home speaking of **delver** slots and **escort** runs, of **sealed-tongue** witnesses and registry clerks who measured a man’s worth in stamps. Tanya heard a career. She heard the promise she had married — steady hunger dressed as duty.

Tarro learned his absences as the same thing: a man climbing in a kingdom that only rewarded climbers.

Tanya learned the shape of worry — not dramatic, not theatrical, just the slow rot of sleeping alone while your husband’s boots are gone and his explanations **sound** like work yet never quite **feel** like it.

When the rumors began — a woman under the patron house, a prisoner with a mouth that never opened — Owain drank them off like poison he pretended was wine, and told himself that was **not** what **charge-sensitive cargo** meant.

And when the world later insisted on a child that wasn’t hers, a bastard brought home with a tidy story and a patron’s name, Owain would remember this threshold.

He would remember what silence cost.

He would remember that he had chosen the keys.
