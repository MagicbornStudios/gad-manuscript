After, Owain washed his hands until they cracked.

The water ran rust-brown at first. Then clean. Then clean again. He still smelled smoke and oil on his skin as if the day had stained him through.

He thought of Tanya waiting in a kitchen that believed itself normal.

He thought of Geraint not born yet, a future weight pressing on his ribs.

He thought of Morgana’s silence — worse than screaming, because screaming would have given him someone to blame besides himself.

When he stepped into the corridor, the guard at the end pretended not to see his face.

Owain walked the route that would look right if anyone asked — past stone that could belong to **any** undercroft tied to church storage — and surfaced where the air carried **beer** again, the public stink of the gate hall clinging to honest men who only ever broke seals on crates.

His hands still smelled like lamp oil and something that was not incense.

Owain was grateful for the guard’s dishonesty. It felt like mercy.
