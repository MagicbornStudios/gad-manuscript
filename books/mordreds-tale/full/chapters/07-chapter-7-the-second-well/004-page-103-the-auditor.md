Jack came when it was almost over — or when Owain could not tell what “over” meant anymore.

He did not enter like a lover. He entered like a man checking inventory: eyes first, then breath, then the small details that meant *still useful* or *ruined*.

Morgana did not look at him.

Jack looked at Owain longer than Owain could stand.

For a heartbeat Owain imagined a different audit — wax and ink, a clerk squinting at a crate list — the kind of inspection that made you feel **seen** in the way a **relic hunter** wanted to be seen: competent, necessary, not disposable.

This was the other kind.

"Good," Jack said, and the word was obscene in how ordinary it sounded. "You’ll do it again when I say."

Owain nodded because his mouth was dry.

On the wall, the lamp trembled once as if the building itself had flinched.
