The lamp was too bright for the size of the room.

Above them, the guild-hall lie still breathed — **register**, **witness**, **descent slot** — the vocabulary Owain used when Tanya asked why the **patron** needed him after dark. *Night roster*, he’d told her once, voice steady as a clerk’s. *Charge runs don’t keep market hours.* It sounded like work. It was the same word Jack used for this.

Oil smoke bit the back of his throat and tried to turn into prayer. Owain set the flame where the Patron’s man had told him to set it — high, cruel, honest — so there would be no corner where a woman could pretend she was not seen.

She was already there.

Morgana sat with her wrists in front of her as if she had decided that dignity was the only currency left. Her eyes lifted once, found his, and did not offer him a word.

Owain felt that silence like a blade on his skin.

He wished she would curse him. He could survive a curse. He didn’t know how to survive her silence.
