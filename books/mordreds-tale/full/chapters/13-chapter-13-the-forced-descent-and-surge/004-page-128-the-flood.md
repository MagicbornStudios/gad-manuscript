Magic did not return like rain.

It returned like a dam breaking inside the body — everywhere at once, too much **yes**.

The **Tree** shuddered. Bark split. Sap hissed into air until the torches looked like jokes.

Merlin’s eyes opened.

Jack reached —

—and Mordred screamed, not from fear, from **overflow**.

The bark band on his wrist **snapped** as if it had never been more than courtesy.

Spells he had whispered in secret, half-smothered, rose to his mouth like vomit and **became** something.

**Gravity** bent around him — his specialty, his instinct — and for one perfect heartbeat he thought: *I can ride this.*

Then the world stopped forgiving him.

The surge was not his.

It was **everyone’s**.

And everyone included the sky.

Above the roots, **Tarro** learned the same lesson in its streets. Relics that had hung as **harmless** things — **paperweights**, **toys**, **blessed** gewgaws sold as **curios** — **woke** with teeth. Panic climbed in **patches**: a **nursery** charm **chewing** plaster, a **merchant’s** luck-stone **splitting** a stall, glass in the **guild** halls **biting** ankles like **petty** gods. The city’s noise became **many** emergencies at once — cover, confusion, and **permission** for every door that had been locked with **old** bargains.
