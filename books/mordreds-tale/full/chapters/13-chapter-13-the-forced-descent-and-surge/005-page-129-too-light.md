Mordred **floated** — first like a leaf, then like a mistake.

Outside Tarro, his gravity cant had always asked for **weight**: anchor, pull, the honest lie of *down*. Here, the surge made him **too light**. The ground forgot his name.

He shot upward.

Clouds tore past — cold, thin, impossible — and panic became a single syllable he **spoke** without thinking. The spell **canceled** the lift.

He **fell**.

Air screamed. He clawed the cant back, the levitation he’d used a hundred times in secret practice — **up** again, **too fast**, flung toward the sky a second time.

Cancel.

Fall.

He tried the spell **slightly different** on reflex — the third time, he **hurtled sideways** as if the world had slapped him, hit earth rolling, **tumbling**, grass and teeth and **noise**.

Darkness.
